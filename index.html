GRC · Game Theory · Law Live

Business Judgment Rule (BJR) Simulator

In Indonesia, BUMN directors can be criminally charged under the Tipikor law if a business decision leads to state loss — even when made in good faith. The result: a Nash Equilibrium of bureaucratic paralysis. This simulator quantifies the invisible narrative inside UU PT Pasal 97(5) and trains decision-makers to reach the Pareto-optimal evidence frontier before taking a business case to the Board.

Game Theory UU PT / Tipikor Principal-Agent ERM Simulation

The BJR Simulator (SubseanivityERM) models the Director's utility function as U = α(Profit) − β(LegalRisk) − γ(CostOfEvidence). Unlike the private sector where β is civil, BUMN directors face β → ∞ under criminal interpretation of "Kerugian Negara." The simulator operationalises BJR immunity (Pasal 97(5) UU No. 40/2007) across 9 evidence clusters — Good Faith, Due Care, Informed Decision, Financial Analysis, Technical Diligence, Legal Compliance, Risk Register, Board Documentation, Stage-Gate — and scores each cluster against a weighted threshold. The target zone (Shield ≥ 75 / Outcome ≥ 60) defines the Pareto-efficient frontier: enough documented evidence to cap β ≈ 0, without over-spending governance budget on gold-plated proof where cost-efficient alternatives suffice. Built from Jensen & Meckling's agency cost theory and Nash's equilibrium framework, applied to the iterated game of Indonesian SOE governance.
